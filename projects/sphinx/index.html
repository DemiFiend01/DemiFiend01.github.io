LLM Sphinx Game

Summary:

Game created for Noc Naukowcow 2025. Judge system implemented using Gemini API. Raycasting mixed with maze generation algorithm.

Description:

When I chose the 'AI in Games' topic for my Noc Naukowcow booth, I immediately knew what I wanted to create. It was the classic Sphinx with their riddles and a twist in the form of an AI helper. Since during the event most of the players would be children, I figured that typos would be made in their answers to the Sphinx's riddles.

One possible solution, is to incorporate a LLM in checking the inputs and evaluating their correctness. Unfortunately, as my hardware does not support CUDA, my model couldn't possibly be local and thus I relied on Gemini API. This was a constraint and a hurdle since I became dependant on the internet connection and the generosity of free tier.

Once I had the model figured out, I moved to the other part of the project. Randomly generated maze in a raycasted environment with the goal right in the middle. I programmed the everything in pygame and tkinter, and one particular tutorial had been a huge help in this endeavour.

The game functions like this - you have two modes and two difficulty levels. You can either play the full game, which is version with the maze and the Sphinx at the end, or you can skip the maze and go straight to the riddles. Speaking of riddles, they were not AI generated, I have either come up with them myself, or been inspired by popular existing options. The role of LLM in all of this is to compare the player's answer to mine and then write a fitting response. Winning is pretty straightforward, you need to score at least two points.

This project had been so much fun and I enjoyed every element of it. The planning, researching LLM models, drawing every resource and obviously coding. I presented my work at a booth as a part of DataScience student research group's field game. It was a blast.
